Alzheimer's Disease

Curriculum

Alzheimer's disease slowly affects memory and thinking. Brain cells become damaged over time, which makes it harder to remember recent events, conversations, and names.

Mental activity and memory exercises can help keep the brain active and support cognitive health.

Brain Games

Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease affects movement and coordination. It happens when certain brain cells that produce dopamine stop working properly.

Exercises that train rhythm, coordination, and reaction time may help support movement control.

Huntington's Disease

Huntington's disease affects thinking, movement, and emotional control.

Cognitive exercises that train focus, pattern recognition, and impulse control may support mental flexibility.
