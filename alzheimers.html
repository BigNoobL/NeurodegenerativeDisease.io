Understanding Alzheimer's Disease

What is Alzheimer's?

Alzheimer's disease is a condition that slowly damages memory and thinking. It affects the brain and makes it harder to remember names, places, and events.

Why does it happen?

Inside the brain, small harmful proteins build up. These proteins damage brain cells that help us store and use memories.

Common Symptoms

  • Forgetting recent conversations
  • Difficulty recognizing familiar places
  • Problems solving simple problems

How Brain Exercises Help

Mental exercises can help keep the brain active. Activities that challenge memory, attention, and focus may help slow cognitive decline.
